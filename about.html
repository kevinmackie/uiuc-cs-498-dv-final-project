About the Visualization

The narrative visualization has been built with a hybrid structure and consists of scenes, annotations, parameters, and triggers, as described below

Hybrid Structure

The narrative visualization should follow one of the three hybrid structures: a martini glass, an interactive slideshow, or a drill down story.

Scenes

The scenes follow a template for consistency, follow an order to best convey the message.

Scene Layout and Visual Structure

Discussion of layout and any visual structure of these scenes.

Overview of Scenes

First scene - shows an overview of SCADA cybersecurity research.

Annotations are used to overlay key security and cybersecurity events onto the graph, to help suggest reasons for the pattern of papers and citations.

Second scene - shows research themes and how they have developed over time.

Third scene - shows key authors, institutions, related to the theme of viruses.

Fourth scene - shows the most influential papers related to viruses in SCADA cybersecurity

Annotations

The annotations should follow a template for visual consistency, should highlight specific data items or trends, and the essay should indicate when they are cleared. Annotations are used on scene 1 to show salient characteristics in the data:
  • Increase in research around 2002
  • Increase in citations around 2010
  • Decrease in citations around 2013
